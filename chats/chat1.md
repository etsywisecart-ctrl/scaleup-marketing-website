Design a high-converting, premium portfolio/business website for **ScaleUp Marketing**, a Digital Transformation Company. Avoid generic "agency template" looks — I want something bold, modern, and distinctive, like top-tier SaaS/tech brands (think Linear, Stripe, Vercel, or a premium ecommerce agency site), NOT a stock Bootstrap/Wix template.

## Company Overview
ScaleUp Marketing helps startups, SMEs, and enterprises **launch, automate, and scale** through two connected offerings:
1. **Services** — Ecommerce (Shopify/eBay/Etsy/Daraz), Web Development, Mobile Apps, SaaS Product Development, CRM/ERP, AI Automation, UI/UX, Digital Marketing, Business Consulting.
2. **Training / Education** — Online & offline (physical institute) classes in Shopify, TikTok Shop, eBay, Etsy, Amazon, AI, and Digital Marketing, including masterclasses, 1:1 mentorship, workshops, and certification.

This dual identity (agency + training academy) should feel unified, not split — like one confident tech-education-commerce brand.

## Brand Assets
- Logo: geometric hexagon mark made of a stylized "S"/growth-arrow shape in a **teal-to-deep-navy gradient** (teal/emerald #2FBF9E at the top, transitioning to deep navy-teal #0E3B52 at the base), paired with a bold navy wordmark "SCALE UP MARKETING" and tagline "**Learn. Launch. Scale.**"
- Build the site around this exact brand palette — do NOT default to a dark/black tech theme. Use a **light, premium background** (white or very light off-white) so the logo's gradient pops as the hero color, the way it does in the logo file.

## Visual Direction
- **Style:** Clean, bright, premium light-mode SaaS/agency aesthetic (think Stripe's light theme or a premium ecommerce-agency site) — NOT dark mode. White/near-white base with the brand's teal-to-navy gradient used boldly for headlines, buttons, icons, and section accents.
- **Color palette:**
  - Primary gradient: Teal/Emerald `#2FBF9E` → Deep Navy-Teal `#0E3B52` (use on the logo mark, primary CTA buttons, key headline words, and section dividers)
  - Base background: White `#FFFFFF` / soft off-white `#F7FAFA`
  - Text: Deep navy `#0E2A38` for headlines and body (matches the wordmark), medium gray `#5B6B72` for secondary text
  - Accent tints: very light teal `#E6F7F3` and light navy `#EAF1F4` for card backgrounds and alternating section bands
- **Typography:** Large, confident display headlines (modern geometric sans — Inter, Satoshi, or General Sans), tight letter-spacing, bold headline weight contrasted with lighter body text. Navy for text; gradient treatment reserved for 1–2 key words per headline.
- **Layout:** Asymmetric, editorial grid — not centered-everything. Bento-grid cards for services/courses on light-tint backgrounds with soft shadows and rounded corners (soft elevation, not glow/glassmorphism, since this is light mode) — a thin gradient top-border accent on cards works well.
- **Motion cues:** Note where scroll-reveal animations, hover states (card lift + gradient border), animated counters (stats), and marquee/logo-scroll (client logos) should go.
- **Imagery:** Abstract tech/ecommerce visuals, dashboard mockups, product shots — avoid cheesy stock photos of people shaking hands.
- **Trust signals throughout:** stats (students trained, projects delivered, revenue generated for clients), client logos, certifications.
- Place the actual logo in the header (left-aligned) and repeat the tagline "Learn. Launch. Scale." near the hero subheadline or footer.

## Critical Instruction — Take Your Time, This Is Not a Template Job
Do not produce a generic "digital agency" or "online store" layout — the kind with a big banner image, three icon-boxes in a row, and a stock testimonial slider. That look is outdated and doesn't convert anymore. Treat this like designing an actual **digital product** (a SaaS dashboard or fintech app landing page), not a brochure website:
- Every section should feel intentional and custom-built for ScaleUp Marketing specifically — not swappable with any other agency's content.
- Avoid: centered hero with generic stock photo, plain 3-column icon grids, boxed testimonial carousels, footer mega-menus that look copy-pasted.
- Prefer: asymmetric compositions, real product/dashboard-style visuals (mock screenshots of a Shopify store, a course dashboard, an analytics panel), layered cards with depth, data/number-driven proof (not just adjectives), and interactive-feeling elements (tabs, toggles, expandable course curriculum, hover-reveal case studies).
- Think and iterate on the layout before finalizing — prioritize a genuinely high-converting structure (clear value prop in 3 seconds, friction-free path to "Book a Call," proof stacked before every ask) over speed or defaulting to familiar patterns.
- The end result should look like it belongs next to Linear, Stripe, or a funded SaaS startup's site — not next to a template marketplace listing.

## Mockups, Icons & Motion — Make Everything Look Real
- **No placeholder/empty boxes anywhere.** Every card, screenshot, or icon slot must show something specific and real-looking — not a gray rectangle, not a generic "chart going up" stock icon, not an unlabeled box.
- **Platform icons must be actual, recognizable brand marks** — when referencing Shopify, TikTok Shop, eBay, Etsy, Amazon, Daraz, Meta/Facebook Ads, WooCommerce, etc., use their real logo/icon (or a faithful recreation of it), not a generic shopping-cart or globe icon standing in for all of them.
- **Dashboard/analytics visuals must look like real product screenshots** — realistic sales numbers, order graphs, ad-performance metrics, revenue trends with actual axis labels and dates — not an abstract squiggly line with no context. If showing a "Shopify store dashboard" mockup, it should look like an actual Shopify admin panel with real-looking product names, order counts, and revenue figures.
- **Proof sections (results/testimonials) need specificity** — real-looking before/after numbers (e.g. "$4,200/mo → $18,600/mo in 5 months"), a named store/brand example, a small mock screenshot of that result (e.g. a revenue graph or order dashboard) next to the testimonial — not just a star rating and a paragraph of text.
- **Include an AI-generated video/animation moment** — call out at least one section (ideally the hero or a dedicated "See It In Action" section) where an embedded looping video/animation shows the product in motion: e.g. a screen-recording-style animation of a Shopify store being built, an AI automation workflow running, or a course dashboard progressing through lessons. Since this is a static design deliverable, mock this as an animated placeholder frame with a play button and a realistic-looking paused frame (not a blank video box) — clearly labeled so it's obvious a real video/animation asset goes there.
- Course/training cards should show a realistic mini-curriculum preview (actual module names and numbers) rather than just a title and "Learn More" button.

## Design Reference — Ramotion-Inspired Editorial Style
Use **ramotion.com** as the core visual reference for layout, typography, and restraint — then apply it through ScaleUp's own teal-to-navy brand color instead of Ramotion's black/white palette. Specifically borrow:
- **Oversized, confident hero typography** — one or two huge editorial-weight headline lines (not a small heading + long paragraph), lots of surrounding negative space, understated CTA button beneath it.
- **Muted, editorial base palette** — keep most of the page in white/off-white/soft gray, and use the brand teal-navy gradient sparingly and intentionally (a headline word, a button, a thin accent shape) rather than covering large areas in color — the restraint is what makes it feel premium, not the amount of color.
- **Case-study-style proof grid** — like Ramotion's "logo + one-line outcome" client grid, build an equivalent for ScaleUp: a grid of client/student result tiles, each showing a brand/store name (or student name), a one-line outcome ("Grew from $4K to $18K/mo in 5 months"), and a small supporting visual — clicking/hovering could reveal more detail.
- **Subtle rounded geometric accent shapes** (soft blobs/polygons in the brand gradient) used as quiet background decoration behind sections — not loud, just texture.
- **Testimonial cards in a clean grid** — round headshot photo, short quote, name, and title/company in a minimal bordered or lightly-shadowed card, arranged in a scrollable row or masonry grid (matching Ramotion's testimonial section), rather than a single big slider.
- **Calm scroll-triggered reveals** — sections and cards should fade/slide in gently on scroll, not flashy — motion should feel refined, not gamey.
- **Organized, information-dense footer** — multiple clean columns of links (Services, Training, Company, Locations/Contact) like Ramotion's footer structure, not a sparse 3-link footer.
- Overall feel: confident, editorial, understated-premium — like a top design studio's site — rather than a loud "buy now" marketing page, while still hitting every conversion requirement below.

## Site Structure & Content

**1. Hero**
- Headline: "Build. Automate. Scale."
- Subheadline: "We engineer digital businesses through Ecommerce, AI, Software, and Growth Strategy — and train the next generation of digital entrepreneurs."
- Dual CTA: "Book a Strategy Call" (primary, gradient button) + "Explore Solutions" (secondary, ghost button)
- Background: animated gradient mesh or subtle particle/grid pattern
- Quick stat strip beneath hero (e.g., students trained, stores launched, years of experience)

**2. About / Positioning**
- One unified ecosystem: training + software + consulting under one roof
- Short, punchy paragraph — not corporate fluff

**3. Services (Agency Side)** — present as a bento grid of clickable cards:
- Ecommerce Development (Shopify, WooCommerce, custom stores)
- Web Development (business sites, SaaS platforms, React/Next.js/Laravel)
- Mobile App Development (iOS/Android, Flutter/React Native)
- SaaS Product Development (MVP to full launch, cloud, billing systems)
- AI Automation
- CRM/ERP Solutions
- UI/UX Design
- Digital Marketing
- Business Consulting

**4. Training / Academy (Education Side)** — visually distinct sub-section (could use a different accent color to separate "Agency" vs "Academy" while staying in the same design system):
- Format toggle: Online Live Classes vs Offline/In-person Institute
- Course tracks as cards: Shopify Mastery, TikTok Shop, eBay/Etsy International Selling, Daraz, AI & Digital Marketing
- Revenue/learning models shown as icons: Regular Classes, Advanced Masterclasses, 1:1 Mentorship, Workshops, Weekly Q&A, Certification, Corporate Training
- Highlight: "Free 3-Day Demo Class" and "Free Consultation Before You Start" as a callout banner
- Course curriculum preview (expandable/accordion) for the flagship Shopify course (11 modules: E-commerce intro, Shopify setup, Products, Shipping, Payments, Product Hunting, China Sourcing, Meta/Ads Setup, Digital Marketing, Order Management, Case Studies)

**5. Process**
- Discover → Strategy → Design → Develop → Launch → Scale
- Horizontal stepper or connected timeline graphic

**6. Why Us**
- Business-first strategy, experienced team, scalable architecture, transparent delivery, long-term partnership
- Present as 4 short cards, icon + one-line each

**7. Industries Served**
- Retail, Healthcare, Education, Real Estate, Manufacturing, Construction, Hospitality, Startups — chip/tag style row

**8. Portfolio**
- Grid/carousel of project & store showcases (placeholder mockups: website, app, Shopify store)

**9. Testimonials**
- Client success stories — carousel or masonry cards with photo, name, result metric (e.g. "3x revenue in 6 months")

**10. FAQ**
- Accordion covering: pricing, timelines, training format, support, technologies, maintenance

**11. Final CTA / Contact**
- Strong closing banner: "Ready to Build Something That Scales?"
- Contact options: phone, email, WhatsApp, office visit — plus a short lead-capture form (Name, Business/Goal, Service Interested In)

## Conversion Requirements
- Sticky header nav with a persistent "Book a Call" button
- Above-the-fold clarity: visitor should understand "agency + academy" within 3 seconds
- Social proof (numbers/logos) visible before first scroll ends
- Every major section ends with a soft or hard CTA
- Mobile-first responsive behavior called out explicitly
